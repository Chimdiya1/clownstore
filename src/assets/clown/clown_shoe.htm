Do YOU Have the Right Shoes (or Tools) for the Job?

I recently started taking dance classes.

  • I want to get in better shape.
  • Have always loved to dance.
  • Seems like a great fit to make me healthier in a pleasurable way.

I'll be honest…the first two weeks have been challenging.

  • My body, especially my hips, are not used to moving like a dancer.
  • They are frozen in the pause zone.
  • (Wish they would thaw and start to move!)

PLUS, I look like a total klutz compared to everyone else.

  • Tripping over my feet.
  • All the spinning sending me flying.
  • And fast twists like being on a Tilt-a-Whirl - never sure what angle I'll end up in.

At the last class, staff finally helped me figure out why I was having so much trouble.

  • It is my shoes.
  • They are fancy running shoes designed to prevent my feet from rolling and limiting side-to-side motion.
  • Great for running - and feel like clown shoes in the dance classes!

What a relief to realize it wasn't me.

  • I was sad when I could not move with ease.
  • Felt like a clod.
  • And started to turn on myself.

It never occurred to me my shoes were wrong.

  • Once I get dance shoes, I'll be spinning with the best of them.
  • My hips will keep unlocking.
  • And my grin light up the room.

Makes me think of chapter leaders: Are you using the right tools to create an amazing (and easy) year for your chapter?
